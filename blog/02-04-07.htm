Thursday, April 11, 2002

Steve and Barb needed a hand moving their boat, Solara, from Delta Marine back to their house on Lake Washington.  I've not been through the Locks before, so I volunteered.


Wheel and telegraph (signal to engine room).


Steam cylinders, cranks and drive shaft below.


Peep hole into the steam boiler.


Piston rods and drive shaft.


This is the ship's thrust bearing.  The thrust along the propeller shaft is transmitted to the hull through this series of plates and collars that wrap around the drive shaft.

 We stopped to view the restoration project on the Virginia V, a 1921 steamer that plied the waters of puget sound as part of Puget Sound's "Mosquito Fleet" of privately run ferries.

The fascinating part here was in seeing an operating steam engine.  I wish I had my video camera - it's much more interesting to see the engine in action than looking at still photos.
Pulling back out into the ship canal after our tour, this tug and barge came right across our path.  Were it not for the cat-like reactions of our captain, we would have surely been run over!

April 13-20, 2002

Spring Break: We head to Tucson to visit our friends, the Zelinskis (click on the link to see a gallery of pictures).

Unfortunately, I didn't get to fly on this trip.  Although I had brought myself up to currency for IFR flight, I didn't feel very safe flying on instruments over the mountains.  And with the weather we were having, there was no way to get out of the WA/OR area w/o crossing mountains IFR.

No matter - I found a pretty reasonable commercial air-fare on Saturday and bought an e-ticket for a flight out Sunday morning.  I must say, it was great to get our of the rain-soaked NW for a week.
