Thursday, April 4, 2002

I finally go flying!  Haven't been up since 9-11.  Got the charts out - oops, they're all out of date - bought all new charts, made sure my medical was current, etc.  Then went to Paine field to shake the rust off my flying skills.  What a gorgeous day.  I had a pretty cool plan for the flight:

  • 3 touch and goes at PAE (satisfies my legal VFR day currency requirement)

  • fly around the house

  • head out to the mountains (Tolt reservoir)

  • fly up to Steven's pass

  • steep bank (45º) turns

  • max glide speed experiment (with and with/out power)

  • Turns and figure eights (ground reference maneuvers)

  • short field landing at PAE

I also brought my GPS to record my ground track.

Best-glide speed, very low power setting, slow prop setting.  Notice only 6 GPH fuel consumption!  I could stay aloft for 12 hours at that rate (and fly 1,400 miles)!
