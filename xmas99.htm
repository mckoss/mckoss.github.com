Christmas, 1999

Merry Christmas, Friends and Family!

The big news this year is that we started the design and construction of our new home on Hunts Point.  Last year, we anticipated a move-in date of Christmas 2000 - we've now pushed that back to May 2001.  For much of the year we've been having weekly meetings with our architects to make enough decisions to hire a contractor and get the project started.  On October 22nd, we were able to break ground!  If you would like to see pictures of the building project as it unfolds, just check out http://mckoss.com/kossres (we even have a web-cam on site for up-to-the-minute status).

We were able to squeeze in a Caribbean cruise during Chris's spring break.  Chris had a blast with his friend Sean from school as they had their own cabin and free-run of the ship.

On June 23rd Deb and I celebrated our 20th wedding anniversary!  We spent the weekend in San Francisco.  A special treat of the trip was attending an MIT alumni dinner at which we were able to meet Buzz Aldrin (the second man to set foot on the moon), Tim Berners-Lee (inventor of the Web), and Bob Metcalfe (inventor of the Ethernet network).  Deb and I sat at Buzz's table.  Having read about the phenomenon of seeing "flashes" of light that was first detected by Buzz during the Apollo 11 mission, we were able to ask him about it over dinner.  Apparently the effect is subtle, but present even with eyes closed.  They believe that gamma radiation occasionally impinges on the retina creating a visible flash of light; we don't see this on earth due to the protection provided by the earth's atmosphere.

We went back to Oklahoma City for Debbie's 20th high-school reunion.  She was able to catch up with old friends and it was especially great to see the bridesmaids from our wedding all together at the reunion picnic.

A highlight of our summer was our week-long visit with friends in Maui.  Deb and I spent one morning kayaking in the open ocean - we even met up with a sea turtle as we paddled back home.  The whole group took a trip along the back road to Hana.  This route cleverly skirts the literally hundreds of hairpin curves that plague the primary route.  We had an adventure diving from high rocks and crawling behind a large waterfall.  We even made a visit to Lindberg's grave.  Somehow I let myself get talked into driving back from Hana on the main road - it really is as bad as I had remembered...hours and hours of turn after turn after turn.  Everything else about the trip was wonderful, thankfully.  Imagine my horror when our friend's daughter Acacia approached me in the morning, "Drive me to Hana, Mike?".  Nooooo!

Chris decided to take up a new instrument this year - the saxophone.  He's picked it up quite quickly and we enjoyed watching his first band concert of the year in November.

Another treat for me has been being able to add to my collection of cryptographic machines.  To date, I've collected a German Enigma, a US Army M-209, and a Swiss NEMA machine.  I hope to display them properly in the library of our new home.

Love to all,
Mike, Deb, and Chris Koss
