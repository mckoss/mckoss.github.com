 Faves.com

I'm currently working at Social Bookmarking site - Faves.com. You can take a look at the things I've been "faving" at:

http://faves.com/users/mike 

In January of 2007, we launched a cool new feature to show the latest Faves that are being shared by Faves.com users.  We call it Buzz.  Our front page shows the top Faves across a wide variety of topics.  We also have a unique page for just about any tag that our users use to categorize their Faves.  The top Buzz pages are:

  • News
  • Video
  • Music
  • Movies
  • Technology
  • Funny

Top reasons Faves.com is cool:

  • Never losing a web site.  Once I've found something, I click the Dot This! button in my browser toolbar and save the link (along with any comments and tags I want to add to it).  I've accumulated over 2,000 interesting web pages since I've been using it (over about 12 months)!  I can search by keyword or tag to find my Faves later.
  • Sharing stuff with friends and family.  It's much easier to share things you find on the web with Faves.com, than it is to write a blog - and a lot less annoying than emailing links.  It's literally two clicks of the mouse, and I've published a nicely annotated note on my Faves.com page.  Now all my friends will see the things I'm currently interested in.
  • Keeping in touch.  Many of my friends and family don't live nearby.  It's not very often that I make the effort to send email out of the blue to an old friend - we don't have that much to talk about that would warrant an email conversation.  With Faves.com, we get to casually share our interests, without the obligation to keep a conversation going.
  • Building a personal Knowledge Network.  My Friends (collectively) find many more things on the web than I do.  Many of them have more expertise than I do.  Now I can tap into what they know, to help me be smarter.  Unlike other social networking products, Faves.com is not a time wasting activity, it actually helps me learn and be more productive (it's also good for time wasting too ... lots of funny videos to watch when I have some down time).
  • Personal Messaging.  Faves.com has made big improvements in helping you communicate with your friends.  Not only can my friends see the Faves I'm creating, but we can comment on each other's Faves and send person-to-person messages using Faves.com's built-in mail system.
